# Demo Script — Meridian Therapeutics, Inc.

---

## The 5-Minute Story

Meridian Therapeutics is 18 months from BLA-enabling activities. Your Phase 2
lead asset is performing, your CMC team is scaling up, and FDA's clock is
ticking. But your quality system wasn't built for this moment.

Right now, controlled documents live in MasterControl. CAPAs get tracked in
spreadsheets or a legacy eQMS that wasn't designed for late-stage GMP. LIMS
data sits in its own silo. When a deviation opens, someone has to manually
stitch together the document version, the batch record, and the corrective
action — across three systems — before your QA team can even begin root cause.

That's not a people problem. That's a systems problem. And it gets worse the
closer you get to BLA.

This platform is one quality system built for BLA-stage GMP. Controlled
documents, CAPA workflows, and lot data — connected, audit-ready, and visible
in a single environment. Let me show you what that looks like in practice.

## Click Path 1: Dashboard — Your Quality Posture at a Glance

**Steps:**
1. Open the application to the Dashboard.
2. Point to the StatsRow across the top (open CAPAs, overdue SOPs, lots in review).
3. Scroll to the DataTable showing recent records across document types.

**Talking Points:**
- "This is what your QA lead sees every morning — no logins to three systems."
- "StatsRow surfaces the numbers that matter for BLA readiness: open actions,
  document expiry, lot disposition status."
- "The DataTable is a unified record view — controlled docs, CAPAs, and lot
  data in one place, not three."

**Aha Beat:**
When the rep highlights that a single row in the DataTable can show a deviation
linked to its CAPA and the affected batch — that cross-system traceability is
the moment.

## Click Path 2: Controlled Document Lifecycle

**Steps:**
1. From the Dashboard DataTable, click into a controlled document record.
2. Show the version history, current approval status, and linked training.
3. Initiate a mock revision to demonstrate the workflow routing.

**Talking Points:**
- "Every SOP, specification, and protocol has a full audit trail — version,
  approver, effective date — exactly what FDA will want in the BLA submission."
- "Routing is configurable. SME review, QA approval, effective date triggers
  training assignments automatically."
- "No more emailing PDFs or printing for wet signatures at 11pm before an audit."

**AI Moment:**
If AI features are enabled: "The system can flag SOPs approaching expiry and
suggest review owners based on prior approval history."

**Aha Beat:**
The reviewer sees that a document change is traceable end-to-end — draft,
redline, approval, training — without leaving the system.

## Click Path 3: CAPA Workflow — From Deviation to Closure

**Steps:**
1. Navigate to the CAPA module from the sidebar.
2. Open an example CAPA record tied to a manufacturing deviation.
3. Walk through: initiation → root cause → action items → effectiveness check.

**Talking Points:**
- "A deviation opens in manufacturing. Within the same system, your QA team
  initiates a CAPA, assigns root cause tasks, and links the affected lot — no
  spreadsheet handoff."
- "Effectiveness checks are scheduled automatically. Nothing falls through the
  cracks 90 days later."
- "For BLA, every open CAPA is a potential inspection finding. This gives you
  real-time visibility into your CAPA backlog and closure rates."

**AI Moment:**
"The system can surface similar historical deviations to accelerate root cause
analysis — reducing the time your team spends searching legacy records."

**Aha Beat:**
The full CAPA lifecycle — from deviation linkage to lot disposition to closure
— is visible in one record. That's your inspection readiness story.

## Click Path 4: Empty State — Onboarding from Your Existing Systems

**Steps:**
1. Toggle to a fresh workspace or filter to a module with no records yet.
2. Show the empty state message.
3. Walk through the data ingestion / integration entry points.

**Talking Points:**
- Empty state copy: "No records yet. Controlled documents, CAPAs, and lot data
  will appear here once ingested from your connected systems."
- "Migration from MasterControl or a legacy eQMS is a structured process — we
  map your existing record types, validate in a staging environment, and cut
  over with full audit trail continuity."
- "You don't start from scratch. You start from where you are."

**Aha Beat:**
The rep acknowledges the real fear — disruption during BLA prep — and shows
that onboarding is phased, validated, and doesn't require a big-bang cutover.

## Anticipated Questions

_No anticipated questions were provided for this engagement. Prep the following
as defaults:_

- **"How does this handle 21 CFR Part 11 compliance?"**
  The platform is designed with Part 11 controls: electronic signatures, audit
  trails, access controls, and system validation documentation available.

- **"What does migration from MasterControl look like?"**
  Structured data mapping, staging validation, and phased cutover. Existing
  audit trails are preserved.

- **"Can this integrate with our LIMS?"**
  Yes — API-based integration is supported. Lot and batch data can be pulled
  directly into the quality record context.

- **"What's the validation package look like for a BLA?"**
  IQ/OQ/PQ documentation is provided. We support your CSV authorship process.
